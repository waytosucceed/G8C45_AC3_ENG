Key Takeaways

Identifying supporting details in an informational text involves looking for factual evidence, statistics, examples, or explanations that reinforce the main idea or argument presented by the author. For instance, in a scientific article about climate change, supporting details might include data on rising temperatures and quotes from climate experts.

In contrast, identifying supporting details in a literary text focuses on how characters, settings, and plot developments illustrate themes or motifs within the narrative.
For example, in a novel like "To Kill a Mockingbird," supporting details might include instances of racial injustice that reinforce the theme of moral growth, such as the trial of Tom Robinson and the perspectives of Scout and Jem. While informational texts rely on objective data to substantiate claims, literary texts use narrative elements to deepen understanding of thematic concepts.
